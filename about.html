The Story Behind Alya

Built for the soldiers
navigating Israel alone

Every year, thousands of young Jews make aliyah and enlist in the IDF without a family support network. We built Alya to be the AI-powered guide they deserve.

The Problem

The system wasn't designed for people without support

Lone soldiers — young men and women who serve in the IDF without a parent or supporting family member nearby — face a combination of challenges that most soldiers never have to deal with: a language barrier, unfamiliar bureaucracy, housing they must find and pay for themselves, and an emotional isolation that no Facebook group fully fixes.

The benefits and rights that Israel offers these soldiers are real and meaningful. But navigating them requires knowing the right office to call, the right Hebrew term to use, and the right document to submit — all without anyone to guide you through.

The Market Gap

Existing solutions are fragmented — soldiers fall through the cracks

Lone soldier support in Israel is not absent — it's scattered. Organizations, Facebook groups, immigration agencies, and welfare officers all play a role. But none of them talk to each other, and none of them are accessible in the soldier's language at 11pm when they need help.

What Alya Does

Three AI tools — one place, five languages

Based on our research and the Jobs-to-be-Done framework, we identified three core problems that every lone soldier faces and built a dedicated AI tool for each.

Rights & Grants
Answer four questions about your status, role, and housing situation. Get a personalized list of benefits you likely qualify for — plus the exact documents you need to apply and what to do next. Available even without an internet connection (offline fallback).
AI Mentor
A conversational AI that speaks your language and remembers your conversation. Ask about army slang (pazam, gibush, mashakit tash), benefits, housing, or just how to navigate a difficult day. If you express distress, the mentor immediately refers you to ERAN (1201) and your welfare NCO — because some things need a human.
Lease Shield
Paste or upload your rental contract (PDF supported). The AI reads every clause and flags what's risky — with a red / yellow / green system and suggested safer wording you can send to your landlord. Built specifically for Israeli rental contracts and lone soldier rights.
